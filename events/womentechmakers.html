Women Techmakers Manila

Engineering the Future

Google believes diversity and inclusion is important for thriving vibrant communities. Focusing on women-inclusion is the first step in a larger effort to diversify the global Google developer community. The GDG community has shown great interest in this initiative, starting with DevFestW last year, so we'll push it forward with this year's Women Techmakers event series.

Let's show more visibility to our Women Techmakers and inspire our next generation of women leaders and Techmakers. Join us as we celebrate also International Women's Month. Please sign up early and save the date. We'll be sending confirmation emails with the details of the venue and how to get there.

Reinabelle Reyes

Research Scientist, Manila Observatory

Astrophysicist, Data scientist, Social Observer. Currently research scientist at Manila Observatory. Will be joining Ateneo de Manila University in April. Previously postdoctoral research fellow at University of Chicago. PhD in Astrophysics from Princeton University (2011). BS in Physics from Ateneo de Manila University (2005), summa cum laude.
Ivygail "Ivy" Ong

Outreach Lead, Open Data Philippines

Ivygail "Ivy" Ong is the Outreach Lead of Open Data Philippines (data.gov.ph). She is also a Project Officer at the Digitization Project Coordination Unit (DPCU), the central node of DBM-led, initiated and supported ICT-related projects and activities that cover internal and government-wide ICT projects and activities. The DPCU is housed in the Office of the Chief Information Officer and Undersecretary Richard "Bon" Moya of the DBM. Prior to working in government, she was the Training Coordinator at the Asian Institute of Management (AIM) TeaM Energy Center for Bridging Leadership and likewise worked with local government units, socio-civic groups, teachers, parents and students to improve the quality of public education as a Program Assistant in Synergeia Foundation.
